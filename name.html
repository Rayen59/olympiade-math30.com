Dr. Rafik Zeraoulia is not only a professor and researcher in mathematics but also a pioneer whose discoveries have applications in various fields, including medicine. His work is a beacon of inspiration, illuminating paths to understanding that were once cloaked in mystery. With a heart set on discovery, he has explored chaotic dynamics and validated Yitang Zhang's Landau-Siegel zero theorem, an achievement detailed in his publication "Chaotic Dynamics and Zero Distribution."

Dr Rafik is a dedicated high school mathematics teacher with over ten years of experience in Algeria. His passion for mathematics drives him to inspire and educate young minds. Like a sculptor chiseling away to reveal hidden beauty, Rafik shapes his students' minds with the tools of knowledge and curiosity. Since 2008, he has actively pursued research in dynamical systems, number theory, probability, and statistics, exploring realms of thought where few dare to venture.

He takes pride in discovering the Zeraoulia sequences, acknowledged in the On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (OEIS). He authored "Math Olympiad for Arabic Countries," a testament to his commitment to nurturing the mathematical minds of tomorrow. As a reviewer for the Journal of Probability and Statistics - Horizon Mathematics, he upholds the standard of scholarly publications, ensuring that knowledge is a flame passed from one generation to the next.
